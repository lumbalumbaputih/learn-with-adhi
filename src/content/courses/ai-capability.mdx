<ModuleHeader number={1} title="Capability before hype" />

AI capability is the ability to define problems, choose the right tool, and verify outputs. It is not only about prompts.

A strong workflow starts with a clear task, relevant context, and success criteria.

<Callout variant="tip" title="Key term">
  <KeyTerm term="human-in-the-loop" definition="Human review and decision making remain part of the process" /> keeps quality and accountability in place.
</Callout>

<ModuleHeader number={2} title="Prompting and verification" />

Effective prompts are structured and specific. You should request format, scope, constraints, and evidence.

Always verify important outputs with source checks. Confidence is not evidence.

<Accordion title="How to review AI output quickly">
  Check factual claims, then inspect logic, then review tone and policy alignment.
</Accordion>

<ModuleHeader number={3} title="Responsible adoption" />

Set guardrails for privacy, bias, and security before scaling usage. Team adoption works best when everyone uses a shared review checklist.

Pilot with small internal tasks, measure improvements, and publish lessons learned.
