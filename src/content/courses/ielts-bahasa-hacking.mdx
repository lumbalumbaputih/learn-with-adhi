<ModuleHeader number={1} title="Language transfer patterns" />

Some errors happen because sentence patterns from Indonesian are transferred directly into English. Recognizing this pattern is the first upgrade.

Practice with short corrections and read each corrected sentence aloud.

<Callout variant="example" title="Example">
  Wrong: "I very like this topic." Correct: "I really like this topic."
</Callout>

<ModuleHeader number={2} title="Reading and listening precision" />

Focus on the main idea before details. IELTS traps often appear when a detail looks similar to a keyword but does not match the question intent.

Use one line notes in your own words to improve recall during listening sections.

<Accordion title="Fast checklist for matching headings">
  Read the full paragraph, identify the central idea, then compare with all options before selecting.
</Accordion>

<ModuleHeader number={3} title="Speaking and writing clarity" />

Aim for clear structure, consistent tense control, and simple but accurate development. Fluency improves when ideas are sequenced before delivery.

Keep one practical template for introductions and conclusions, then adapt naturally to each topic.
